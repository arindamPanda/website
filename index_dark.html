About the Researcher

My research centers on the non-equilibrium statistical mechanics of active matter. Specifically, I study active polymers—systems that bridge the gap between inanimate materials and living matter. By integrating Brownian dynamics simulations with analytical theory, I investigate how internal activity fundamentally alters the conformational, mechanical, and rheological fingerprints of polymer systems.

Analytical Analysis of the Conformational and Rheological Properties of Flexible Active Polar Linear Polymers

This study develops an analytical framework to characterize the conformational and rheological properties of flexible Active Polar Linear Polymers (APLPs) under shear flow. It reveals that activity leads to quantitatively distinct behavior, including enhanced transverse shrinkage and amplified shear-thinning, with scaling exponents that align with previous simulation findings.

Folding-Unfolding Transition of Active Polymer on the Reconfiguration of Bidirectional Tangential Active Force

This paper examines how bidirectional tangential active forces influence the conformational and dynamic properties of active polymers. It reveals that the interplay of force directionality and reversal rates can drive polymers through compression, re-swelling, and distinct dynamic regimes, bridging behaviors previously observed in unidirectional and random active polymer models.

2

Activity-Enhanced Shear Thinning of Flexible Linear Polar Polymers

The tangential polar activity in flexible linear polymers dramatically enhances shear-thinning behavior, leading to a viscosity reduction scaling with a \(Wi_{Pe}^{-4/3}\) exponent, alongside amplified transverse shrinkage and altered tumbling dynamics, which are distinct from passive polymers. The study leveraged both coarse-grained simulations and an analytical model to characterize these unique rheological signatures.

Characteristic Features of Self-avoiding Active Brownian Polymers Under Linear Shear Flow

This study used Brownian dynamics simulations to investigate how active noise and excluded-volume interactions modify the conformational and rheological properties of flexible polymers under linear shear flow. We found new power-law scalings for polymer compression, alignment, and shear thinning in active systems, along with a non-monotonic zero-shear viscosity driven by excluded-volume effects.
